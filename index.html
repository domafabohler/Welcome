About me

Passionate about driving social change and fostering sustainable development, I am a dedicated student at Brigham Young University (BYU) in Provo, currently majoring in Pre-Business. I aspire to join the Entrepreneurial Management Program with a minor in Global and Community Impact, aiming to become a social entrepreneur active in international development and creating impactful solutions that address global challenges. Born in Germany, I have always been driven by a desire to make a difference. My professional journey began with two years of volunteer service in France, where I honed my leadership and interpersonal skills. Since September 2022, I have been working part-time at the IFES Management Office. In a short span, I have earned a reputation as a 'quick study' for my ability to quickly understand, carefully analyze, and continuously improve processes. At IFES, I have been instrumental in advancing the organization’s sustainability goals. My role involves researching, recommending, and communicating strategies for sustainability and environmental responsibility. I thrive in this dynamic, global environment where collaboration and innovation are key.

ISO 20121 Training

One-year Training Program Towards Sustainable Event Management According to ISO 20121.

I am excited to share my involvement in the development and expansion of the ISO 20121 Training Program by IFES and fwd., a transformative initiative designed to foster sustainability within the events industry. ISO 20121 is the premier international management standard for sustainable event management, providing a framework that ensures long-term value creation and operational excellence.

Key Highlights:

  • Training: The convoy curriculum Marko Roscher (fwd.) and I developed offers a one-year training experience, including two immersive workshops and ten guided sessions, providing step-by-step guidance tailored to the needs of participating organizations.
  • Expert-Led Sessions: With Marko and I being directly from the events industry particpant learn from people with direct sector knowledge, addressing specific challenges and pain points.
  • Cost-Cutting Solution: The innovative training approach cutts costs of implementation trainings by about 75% and additionally provides participants with templates, collaborative learning and international networking opportunities.

This project underscores our commitment to promoting sustainability in the events sector, providing companies with the tools and knowledge to integrate sustainable practices effectively. Through this initiative, we aim to help organizations achieve ISO 20121 certification, enhancing their credibility and operational resilience in a rapidly evolving industry. Read more at ifesnet.com

  • Category: Sustainability Management

Red Hand Day

International Action Day against the use of Child Soldiers

As the Initiator and Leader of the Red-Hand-Day project at my high school, I spearheaded a large-scale charity initiative aimed at raising awareness and combating the use of child soldiers worldwide. This impactful project, conducted during my high school years, involved extensive coordination, strategic planning, and community engagement.

Key Highlights:

  • Leadership & Coordination: Organized and led the project across six schools, successfully engaging over 6,500 participants.
  • Event Planning & Execution: Planned and executed various events and activities, including presentations, workshops, and political panel discussions, ensuring widespread participation and impactful outcomes.
  • Collaboration & Networking: Worked closely with local schools, regional student bodies, state politicians, and international organizations to amplify the project's reach and effectiveness.
  • Communication & Public Speaking: Delivered presentations and speeches to large audiences, effectively communicating the cause and inspiring others to join the movement.
  • Awareness & Advocacy: Raised significant awareness about the issue of child soldiers, mobilizing students, teachers, and community members to take action.

This project not only fostered a deep sense of social responsibility and advocacy within the community but also strengthened my leadership, organizational, and communication skills. The success of Red-Hand-Day stands as a testament to the power of collective action and the importance of standing up for human rights.
